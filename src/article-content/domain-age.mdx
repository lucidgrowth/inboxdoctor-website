### Domain Age: The Trust Factor of Time

**Definition:** Domain Age refers to the length of time a domain has been registered and active, calculated from its creation date. It’s a critical trust signal for email systems, showing how long your domain has been established and impacting its reputation with spam filters and email providers.

### Domain Age 101: The Basics of Time-Built Credibility

Think of Domain Age as your domain’s seniority badge—the longer it’s been active, the more credible it appears. When a domain is registered, its creation date marks the start of its journey. Email providers and spam filters use this age to gauge trustworthiness: older domains are often seen as reliable, while new ones may be flagged as risky until they prove themselves.

### Benefits of Domain Age for Email Deliverability

A well-aged domain enhances email deliverability by building trust and reducing suspicion. Here’s why Domain Age is essential:

- **Boosts Sender Reputation:** Older domains signal stability, earning higher trust scores from email providers and improving inbox placement.
- **Reduces Spam Flags:** New domains (less than 30 days old) are often flagged by spam filters as potential threats—longer Domain Age lowers this risk.
- **Improves Delivery Rates:** Email servers favor domains with a history (e.g., 90+ days), ensuring your emails (marketing, transactional, etc.) land in the inbox.
- **Signals Legitimacy:** A domain active for years is less likely to be linked to phishing compared to a freshly registered one.
- **Supports Authentication Trust:** Aged domains with consistent SPF/DKIM setups gain more credibility, enhancing deliverability.
- **Lowers Rejection Risks:** Servers are less likely to block emails from domains with established age, ensuring smooth delivery.
- **Builds Recipient Confidence:** Recipients trust emails from older domains, reducing the chance of spam complaints.
- **Counters Blacklist Triggers:** New domains are often blacklisted—longer Domain Age helps avoid automatic suspicion.
- **Aligns with Spam Filter Checks:** Filters prioritize Domain Age as a trust factor; older domains pass scrutiny more easily.
- **Future-Proofs Deliverability:** As email security tightens, Domain Age will remain a key metric for proving reliability.
